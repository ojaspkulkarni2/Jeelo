## Plot
### Travel:
1. December 1916 convention of the Indian National Congress in **Lucknow**
2. Returned to his ashram near **Ahmedabad**
3. Told Shukla to meet him in **Kolkata**
4. **Patna** in Bihar where they lived at Rajendra Prasad's house
5. **Muzzafarpur** in the house of Professor Malkani
6. **Champaran**
7. **Motihari**: the capital of Champaran

### Cause & Effect
##### 1. Gandhi visited the secretary of the British landlords association.
He was told that they could give no information to an outsider. He answered that he was no outsider.
##### 2. Gandhi called on the British official commissioner of the Tirhut division in which the Champaran district lay.
The commissioner proceeded to bully Gandhi and advised him to forthwith leave Tirhut.
##### 3. Gandhi proceeded to Motihari, the capital of Champaran.
1. Several lawyers accompanied him.
2. At the railway station, a vast multitude greeted Gandhi.
3. He went to a house, and using it as headquarters, continued his investigations.
##### 4. A report came in that a peasant had been maltreated in a nearby village.
Gandhi decided to go and see; the next morning, he started out on the back of an elephant.
##### 5. The police superintendent's messenger told Gandhi to return. When Gandhi agreed, he was given a notice to quit Champaran.
Gandhi signed a receipt for the notice and wrote on it that he would disobey the order.
##### 6. Gandhi received a summons to appear in court the next day.
1. He remained awake all night.
2. He telegraphed Rajendra Prasad to come from Bihar with influential friends.
3. He sent instructions to the ashram.
4. He wired a full report to the Viceroy.
##### 7. The spontaneous demonstration of people in thousands around the courthouse was the beginning of their liberation of fear from the British.
1. The government was baffled
2. The prosecutor requested the judge to postpone the trial
3. The authorities wished to consult their superiors
##### 8. Gandhi protested against the delay and read a statement pleading guilty.
The magistrate announced that he would pronounce sentence after a two hour recess and ask Gandhi to furnish bail for those 120 minutes. Gandhi refused and the judge released him without bail.
##### 9. Gandhi was supported by many lawyers.
Civil disobedience triumphed.
### Gandhi's words
1. "I have to be in Kolkata on such-and-such a date. Come and meet me and take me from there."
2. "It was an extraordinary thing in those days for a government professor to harbour a man like me.
3. "I have come to the conclusion that we should stop going to law courts. Taking such cases to the courts does little good. Where the peasants are so crushed and fear-stricken, law courts are useless. The real relief for them is to be free of fear."
4. "The Commissioner proceeded to bully me and advised me forthwith to leave Tirhut."
5. "What I did was a very ordinary thing. I declared that the British could not order me about in my own country."
6. " You think that in this unequal fight it would be helpful if we have an Englishman on our side. This shows the weakness of your heart. The cause is just and you must rely upon yourselves to win this battle. You should not seek a crop in Mr Andrews because he happens to be an Englishman."
## Questions
### How did Shukla convince Gandhi to come to Champaran?
1. He requested Gandhi to come
2. He accompanied Gandhi everywhere till he agreed
3. For weeks, he didn't leave Gandhi's side
4. He begged Gandhi to "Fix a date"
### How was Gandhi treated by Rajendra Prasad's servants?
1. They took him as another peasant, but let him stay on the grounds
2. He was not permitted to draw water from the grounds since they didn't know if he was an untouchable
### How did the people react to Gandhi's arrival?
1. The nature of his mission spread quickly through Muzzafarpur and to Champaran
2. Sharecroppers from Champaran came to see him
3. Muzzafarpur lawyers called on him to brief him
### Describe the situation at Champaran.
1. Arable land was divided into large estates owned by Englishmen and worked by Indian tenants
2. The chief commercial crop was indigo
3. Landlords compelled tenants to plant 15% of their fields with indigo to be paid as rent
4. The landlords hired thugs for those who resisted and engaged lawyers
### Why did the officials feel powerless without Gandhi's cooperation?
1. He helped them regulate the crowd
2. He was polite and friendly
3. He was giving them concrete proof that their might, hitherto dreaded and unquestioned, could by challenged by Indians
#### Why did Gandhi agree to a settlement of 25% refund to the farmers?
1. The amount of refund was less important than the fact that the landlords had been obliged to surrender part of their money and prestige.
2. Now the peasant saw that he had rights and defenders. He learned courage.
3. Events justified Gandhi's position.
#### How did Gandhi change Champaran?
1. Many volunteers came from parts of India
2. Primary schools were opened in six villages
3. Gandhi got a doctor to volunteer his services for 6 months
4. He asked Kasturbai to talk to women about the state of their clothes
#### Describe the doctor's health services at Champaran.
1. Three medicines were available — castor oil, quinine, and sulphur ointment.
2. Coated tongue: castor oil
3. Malaria fever: quinine + castor oil
4. Skin eruptions: ointment + castor oil
