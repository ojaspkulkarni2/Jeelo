## Part 1
### Questions
#### Describe Saheb.
1. Saheb-e-Alam left his home long ago
2. He spends his morning scrounging garbage dumps for coins
3. His home in Dhaka was swept away by storms
4. He doesn't go to school as there is none in his neighbourhood
#### What are the boys' opinions on chappals?
1. One boy's mother didn't bring them down from the shelf
2. His friend says he would throw them off
3. Another boy wants shoes but could never afford them
#### What is the story a man from Udipi once told the narrator?
1. He would go to the temple (where his father was a priest) as a young boy to pray for shoes
2. Thirty years later, the narrator saw his son in a grey uniform wearing shoes
3. But many ragpickers remain shoeless
#### What explanation does the author offer for the children not wearing footwear?
1. One explanation is that it is not poverty but a tradition to stay barefoot
2. The author believes that this an excuse to explain poverty
#### Describe the life of ragpickers.
1. They live in structures of mud, with roofs of tin or tarpaulin devoid of basic amenities
2. They live without an identity, but only on ration cards
3. Wherever they find food, they pitch their tents
4. Garbage to them, is gold. It is their daily bread.
#### How does Saheb's life change on getting a stable job?
1. His face has lost the carefree look
2. The steel canister seems heavier than the plastic bag he'd carry
3. He is no longer his own master
