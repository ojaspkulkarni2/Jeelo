## Selma Lagerlöf
1. Swedish writer with many translated stories
2. Universal theme: the essential goodness in a human being can be awakened through understanding and love
3. Setting: The mines of Sweden, rich in iron ore
## Characters
### The Rattrap Seller
1. His clothes were in rags
2. His cheeks were sunken
3. Hunger gleamed in his eyes
4. He had to resort to begging and petty thievery
### The Crofter
1. An old man without a wife or a child who felt lonely
2. Used to be a crofter at Ramsjö Ironworks
3. Naïve yet generous to the rattrap seller
4. Owned a cow that gave milk for the creamery every day
### The Ironmaster
1. A very prominent ironmaster whose greatest ambition was to ship out good iron to the market
2. Watched the forge both day and night to see the work was done as well as possible
3. Couldn't say no to his daughter when she let the peddler stay
### Edla Willmansson
1. The ironmaster's daughter
2. Not at all pretty, but modest and quite shy
3. Kind to the peddler in spite of their differences

## Questions 
### What was the peddler's amusing idea?
1. He was struck by the thought that the whole world was a big rattrap
2. It offered happiness the same way a rattrap offered cheese
3. Since he thought the world had never been kind to him
4. It became a cherished pastime of his
### How did the crofter treat the peddler?
1. He was happy to get someone to talk to in his loneliness and let him in
2. He gave him porridge for supper
3. He gave him a large amount of tobacco for his pipe
4. He got out an old pack of cards and played mjölis till bedtime.
### Describe the peddler's journey through the woods
1. During the first hours, it caused him no difficulty
2. He tried to walk in one direction, but the paths twisted back and forth
3. He realised he'd been walking in circles
4. The whole forest closed in upon him like an impenetrable prison
5. He heard a sound and walked towards the Ramsjö Ironworks
### Why did the ironmaster invite the rattrap seller to his home? How did he do so?
1. He mistook the rattrap seller to be an old friend in the dark
2. He repeatedly asked him to come back home saying that there were only 2 people home
3. He sent his daughter to invite him home
### What made the peddler accept Edla Willmansson's invitation?
1. She looked at him compassionately with her heavy eyes
2. She spoke in a friendly manner that the rattrap peddler must have felt confidence in her
### What did the peddler say in his defence when it was clear that the ironmaster had made a mistake?
1. He took a step forward and struck the table with his fist
2. He explained his ideology of the world being a rattrap
3. He said that if the ironmaster gets him locked up, the same will happen to him someday
### Why did Edla still entertain the peddler even after she knew the truth about him?
1. That morning she had felt so happy when she thought about how homelike and Christmassy she was going to make things for the poor wretch
2. She could not get away from the idea all at once
