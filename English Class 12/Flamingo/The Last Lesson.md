## Alphonse Daudet
1. French novelist and short story writer
2. The story is set in the days of the Franco-Prussian War
3. France was defeated by Prussia, led by Bismarck
4. In this story Alsace-Lorraine have passed down into Prussian hands
## Characters
### Franz
1. A lively young boy shown to initially dislike school
2. Towards the end of the story he realises its importance
3. The story is written from his point of view
### M Hamel
1. The French teacher who taught in the town
2. He was strict but loved the students and his subject
3. He believed that when a people are enslaved, as long as they hold fast to their language, it's as if they hold the key to their prison
## Plot
### Notes
1. What was Franz expected to be prepared with for school: Participles
2. What had been put up on the bulletin board: German would be taught in schools
## Questions
### Describe the day of the last lesson.
1. It was so warm and so bright
2. The birds were chirping at the edge of the woods
3. The Prussian soldiers were drilling in the open fields at the back of the sawmill
4. It was all more tempting to Franz than the participles to be taught in class and he wished to run away and spend the day outside
### How did school usually begin?
1. There was a great bustle which could be heard out in the street
2. The opening and closing of desks
3. Lessons repeated in unison, with our hands over our ears to help us understand better
4. The teacher's great ruler rapping on the table
### What did Franz notice unusual about the school that day?
1. M Hamel was unusually kind and dressed well
2. The school seemed strange and solemn
3. The village people sat on the back benches
### Describe M Hamel on the day of the last lesson.
1. Beautiful green coat
2. Frilled shirt
3. Little black cap, all embroidered
4. He never wore this except on inspection or prize days
### Who was sitting in the back benches?
1. Old Hauser with his three cornered hat
2. The former mayor
3. The former postmaster
4. Several others besides
### How did Franz react on learning it was his last French lesson
1. He felt sorry for
	1. not learning his lessons
	2. for seeking birds' eggs
	3. for going sliding on the Saar
2. Considered his heavy books as old friends
3. Forgot about M Hamel's ruler and crankiness
### Why were the old people attending the lecture
1. Sorry that they hadn't gone to school more
2. Thanking their master for 40 years of service
3. Showing their respect for their country
### Why did M Hamel feel sorry?
1. Franz's parents preferred to put him to work on a farm
2. He often sent Franz to water his flowers instead of learning lessons
3. When he wanted to go fishing, he'd give students a holiday
### Describe the lesson in writing.
1. M Hamel had new copies written in a beautiful round hand
2. It was quiet when everyone set to work
3. The only sound was the scratching of pens on paper
4. Some beetles flew in, but they went unnoticed
### How had the place changed in M Hamel's forty years of teaching?
1. The desks and benches had been worn smooth
2. The walnut trees in the garden were taller
3. The hopvine  that he had planted himself twined about the windows to the roof
### How did the last lesson end?
1. The clock struck twelve
2. The trumpets of the Prussian soldiers sounded
3. M Hamel tried to address the class but choked
4. He wrote on the board, "Viva La France"
5. He dismissed the students with a wave of his hand
### What changes did the order from Berlin cause that day
1. M Hamel had to leave the country and could no longer teach French
2. The students began feeling love for French that they didn't feel before
3. It raised patriotism among the village people
