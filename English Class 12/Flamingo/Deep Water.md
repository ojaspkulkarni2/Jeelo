## William Douglas
1. Was born in Maine, Minnesota
2. Graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in English and Economics
3. Spent two years teaching high school in Yakima
4. Decided to pursue a legal career
5. Met Franklin D. Roosevelt at Yale, and became his adviser and friend
6. He was a leading advocate of individual rights
7. Retired as the longest serving Justice in the history of the court with a term lasting 36 years
## Plot
1. He had been scared of water from his early years
2. He tried to overcome this fear at the pool in the YMCA
3. He had a near death experience
4. He was scared by water from that day on
5. He tried swimming yet once more as an adult
6. He overcame his fear of water
7. "All we have to fear, is fear itself", Franklin D. Roosevelt
## Questions
### Why did the narrator have an aversion to water from the beginning?
1. His father would take him to surf
2. He would hang on as the waves knocked him down
3. He would be buried in water
4. His father would laugh, but he felt terror
### Describe the older boy
1. Probably 18 years old
2. Thick hair on his chest
3. Beautiful physical specimen with legs and arms that showed muscles
4. A bully who wanted to mess with the little boy
### What did the older boy do to the narrator?
1. He called out to the narrator as "Skinny"
2. He picked up the narrator
3. He tossed him in the deep end of the water
### Describe the narrator's experience in the water.
1. Swallowing water, he went down to the bottom
2. Frightened, he decided to jump as his feet touched the ground
3. The jump didn't help. On opening his eyes, he saw dirty yellow water
4. He suffocated and choked water at the surface
5. He went down again. His lungs ached, and heart throbbed.
6. He felt a stark terror paralyze him, but bounced off the water
7. The jump made no difference. He couldn't move.
8. He sucked for air and got water, then was rescued
### How did the narrator feel after being rescued?
1. He lay on his stomach, vomiting
2. He walked home, weak and trembling
3. He shook and cried on his bed
4. He didn't eat that night
5. For days, a haunting fear was in his heart
6. The slightest exertion upset him, making him wobbly in the knees and sick to his stomach
7. He never went back in the pool, feared water and avoided it
### How did the experience affect the narrator later on in life?
1. He always wanted to get in water but whenever he did, the terror would come back
2. His legs would be paralyzed and an icy horror would grab his heart.
3. This handicap stayed with him for:
	   1. Wading in the Tieton or Bumping River
	   2. Bathing in Warm Lake of the Goat Rocks
	   3. In canoes on Maine lakes fishing for landlocked Salmon
	   4. Bass fishing in New Hampshire
	   5. Trout fishing on the Deschutes
	   6. Metolius in Oregon
	   7. Fishing for Salmon on the Columbia
	   8. At Bumping Lake in the Cascades
### How did the instructor help the narrator with his fear?
1. He used a belt and pulley contraption to help
2. He would relax his rope and slowly the tension the narrator felt then, began to slack
3. He taught the narrator to put his face under the water and exhale
4. He held him at the edge of the pool and had him just kick with his legs
### How did the narrator prove to himself that he could swim?
1. He went to Lake Wentworth in New Hampshire, dived off a dock and swum two miles
2. He swam
	1. The crawl
	2. Breast stroke
	3. Side stroke
	4. Back stroke
3. He eliminated residual doubts by swimming Warm Lake both ends.
