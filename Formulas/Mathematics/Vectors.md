## Scalar Triple Product
#### Basics
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{a} &= a_1 \hat{i} + a_2 \hat{j} + a_3 \hat{k} \\
\mathbf{b} &= b_1 \hat{i} + b_2 \hat{j} + b_3 \hat{k} \\
\mathbf{c} &= c_1 \hat{i} + c_2 \hat{j} + c_3 \hat{k}
\end{aligned}
$$
$$
[\mathbf{a\ b\ c}] =
\begin{vmatrix}
a_1 & a_2 & a_3\\
b_1 & b_2 & b_3 \\
c_1 & c_2 & c_3
\end{vmatrix}
$$
$$[\mathbf {a \ b \ c}] = Volume \ of \ parallelopiped$$
$$\frac{1}{6}[\mathbf {a \ b \ c}] = Volume \ of \ tetrahedron$$
#### Properties
$$ \mathbf {a \cdot (b \times c)} = \mathbf {(a \times b) \cdot c} $$
$$ [\mathbf {a \ b \ c}] = [\mathbf {b \ c \ a}] = [\mathbf {c \ a \ b}] $$
$$ [t \mathbf{a \ a \ b}] = 0 $$
$$ [l \mathbf a \ m \mathbf b \ n \mathbf c] = lmn [\mathbf {a \ b \ c}]$$
$$ [\mathbf {a  \pm  l\ \ b \ c}] = [\mathbf {a \ b \ c}] + [\mathbf {l \ b \ c}] $$
If $[\mathbf {a \ b \ c}] = 0$ then
1. $\mathbf a = 0, \mathbf b = 0, \mathbf c = 0$
2. Any two vectors are parallel
3. All three vectors are coplanar
#### Results
$$ [\mathbf{a + b \ \ b + c \ \ c + a}]= 2[\mathbf {a  \ b \ c}] $$
$$ [\mathbf {a \ b \ c}]^2 = 
\begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf a \cdot \mathbf a & \mathbf a \cdot \mathbf b & \mathbf a \cdot \mathbf c\\
\mathbf b \cdot \mathbf a & \mathbf b \cdot \mathbf b &  \mathbf b \cdot \mathbf c \\
\mathbf c \cdot \mathbf a & \mathbf c \cdot \mathbf b &  \mathbf c \cdot \mathbf c
\end{vmatrix}
$$

## Vector Triple Product
#### Basics
$$ \mathbf{a \times (b \times c)} = (\mathbf{a \cdot c}) \mathbf {b} - (\mathbf{a \cdot b}) \mathbf {c} $$
#### Properties
$$ \mathbf{a \times (b \times c)} + \mathbf{b \times (c \times a)} + \mathbf{c \times (a \times b)} = 0$$
$$[\mathbf{a \times b} \ \ \mathbf{b \times c} \ \ \mathbf{c \times a}] = [\mathbf {a \ b \ c}]^2$$
## Scalar and Vector Product of 4 Vectors

1. Vector Product = $\mathbf{(a \times b) \times (c \times d)}$
2. Scalar Product =  $\mathbf{(a \times b) \cdot (c \times d)}$
$$Scalar \ Product = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{a \cdot c} & \mathbf{a \cdot d}\\
\mathbf{b \cdot c} & \mathbf{b \cdot d}
\end{vmatrix}$$
$$ \mathbf{(a \times b) \cdot (c \times d)} + \mathbf{(b \times c) \cdot (a \times d)} + \mathbf{(c \times a) \cdot (b \times d)} = 0$$
## Reciprocal System of Vectors
#### Basics
$$\mathbf a' = \frac{b \times c}{\mathbf{[a \ b \ c]}}, \ \mathbf b' = \frac{c \times a}{\mathbf{[a \ b \ c]}}, \ \mathbf c' = \frac{a \times b}{\mathbf{[a \ b \ c]}}$$
#### Properties
$$ \mathbf {a \cdot a'} = 1 $$
$$ \mathbf {b \cdot b'} = 1 $$
$$ \mathbf {c \cdot c'} = 1 $$
$$ \mathbf {a' \cdot c'} = 0 $$
$$ \mathbf {a' \cdot b'} = 0 $$
$$ \mathbf {c' \cdot a'} = 0$$
$$ [\mathbf {a' \ b' \ c'}] = \frac{1}{\mathbf{[a \ b \ c]}} $$
1. If $\mathbf{a', b', c'}$ is the reciprocal system of vectors to $\mathbf{a, b, c}$  then $\mathbf{a, b, c}$ is the reciprocal system of vectors to $\mathbf{a', b', c'}$
2. $\hat i, \hat j, \hat k$ are its own reciprocal system of vectors
3. Any vector $\mathbf r$ can be written as:
$$ \mathbf r' = \mathbf{(r \cdot a')a + (r \cdot b')b + (r \cdot c')c}  \ \  or \ \ \mathbf r' = \mathbf{(r \cdot a)a' + (r \cdot b)b' + (r \cdot c)c'}  $$
